# CE Registration Platform Competitive Landscape Research
**Prepared for:** Matt, Program Developer at Kirkwood Community College  
**Date:** February 17, 2026  
**Purpose:** Strategic evaluation of building a modern, AI-native CE registration platform  

---

## Executive Summary

The community college continuing education (CE) registration software market is **fragmented but consolidating rapidly**, dominated by one heavyweight (Modern Campus) after its 2022 acquisition of the #2 player (Augusoft/Lumens). The market serves ~1,167 community colleges managing 4.1 million non-credit students ‚Äî and most are stuck on legacy systems that haven't fundamentally changed in 15+ years.

**The opportunity is real.** Modern Campus is now a monopolist charging premium prices with a product that reviewers consistently call "counter-intuitive," "a work in progress," and full of "workarounds." The #2 and #3 alternatives (Enrole, CampusCE) are small, underfunded challengers. Nobody has built an AI-native platform for this space. The total addressable market for software serving community college CE divisions is conservatively **$50‚Äì75M ARR** at current price points, with room to expand.

## 1. Market Context

### Community College Numbers (2024)
- **1,026** AACC member-eligible community colleges (down slightly from 1,038 in 2023)
- **1,167** total public and independent community colleges in the US
- **~1,600** when branch campuses included
- **10.5 million** total headcount enrollment (fall 2024)
  - 6.4 million credit students
  - **4.1 million non-credit students** (the CE market)
- Non-credit enrollment grew **11.7%** year-over-year in fall 2024 ‚Äî fastest growing segment

### CE Market Size
- US continuing education market: **$66.91 billion** in 2024, projected to **$95.98 billion by 2030** (6.2% CAGR)
- Modern Campus stat: **48% of CE leaders don't know their own enrollment numbers** ‚Äî speaks to the data chaos in the market
- Augusoft alone processed **$3 billion+ in CE transactions** before acquisition (hundreds of schools, 8M+ students registered)

### TAM Estimate for Registration Software
- ~800‚Äì900 community colleges with active CE/workforce divisions needing dedicated software
- Enterprise contracts (Destiny One tier): $25,000‚Äì$75,000+/year (estimated; Modern Campus doesn't publish pricing)
- Mid-market contracts (Lumens/Enrole tier): $10,000‚Äì$30,000/year
- Budget-market contracts (ACEware, CourseStorm): $3,000‚Äì$12,000/year
- **Rough TAM for community college CE software: ~$50‚Äì75M/year ARR** (at current pricing)
- Opportunity to expand TAM with AI-powered upsells, marketing automation modules, analytics, corporate training portals

## 3. Pain Points Deep Dive

### What CE Directors Actually Complain About

**1. UI/UX is Stuck in 2005**
- Destiny One: "The UI can be terribly counter-intuitive" (Capterra)
- ACEware: Desktop-first software, bolt-on web
- Banner CE: Not designed for web-based self-service at all
- Students expect Amazon-quality registration; they get DMV quality

**2. No Real Mobile Experience**
- Most CE platforms are responsive but not mobile-first
- Students increasingly register on phones; many legacy platforms fail here
- CourseStorm does this well; Destiny One does not

**3. Reporting is a Nightmare**
- 48% of CE leaders "do NOT know their enrollment numbers" (Modern Campus's own stat ‚Äî remarkable)
- Destiny One complaints: Can't pull instructor pay vs. enrollment revenue reports easily
- Can't pull group/invoice reports effectively
- Data lives in silos ‚Äî CE system, Banner, Salesforce, Excel all have different numbers
- No predictive analytics or program-level insights

**4. No Integrated Marketing Automation**
- Email newsletters: usually manual or requires a separate tool (Mailchimp, Constant Contact)
- No automated abandoned registration sequences
- No SMS/text messaging for class reminders, registration nudges, waitlist notifications
- No behavior-based triggers ("student browsed cybersecurity courses 3x ‚Äî send email")
- CourseStorm has basic marketing email tools; none have true automation

**5. Corporate/B2B Training Portal is Weak or Missing**
- Workforce development requires: corporate account management, purchase orders, bulk registration, invoice billing
- Destiny One has Corporate Engagement Manager module ‚Äî but reviewers note it's complex
- Most other platforms can't handle B2B at all
- Companies want a portal where HR can manage their employees' training

**6. Youth Programs Are a Pain**
- Community education runs youth camps, after-school programs
- Most CE platforms require workarounds for guardian registration, age restrictions, emergency contacts
- CourseStorm handles this relatively well; others don't

**7. The Workaround Culture**
- "My teams (registrar, marketing, IT, academics) are constantly figuring out workarounds because Destiny does not have the features necessary to support their work." ‚Äî SoftwareAdvice
- ACEware: Known for users who hack the system with custom reports
- Staff waste hours weekly on manual processes that should be automated

**8. Upgrades Break Things**
- Destiny One: "Upgrades can break procedures that were previously working" ‚Äî Capterra
- This is a sign of poor software engineering; tech debt accumulated over 20 years

**9. Implementation Takes Forever**
- Destiny One implementations: 6‚Äì12 months typical
- Enrole pitches 60‚Äì90 days as a differentiator ‚Äî and it's working
- ACEware SaaS: unknown

**10. Vendor Lock-In + Pricing Power**
- Once you're on Destiny One, switching costs are enormous (data migration, retraining)
- Modern Campus knows this and prices accordingly
- Small programs can't afford Destiny One; they're stuck on ACEware or spreadsheets

## 5. Strategic Questions Answered

### Is There a Dominant Player?
**Yes ‚Äî Modern Campus is dominant after the Augusoft acquisition, but not unassailable.**
- They now control approximately 60‚Äì70% of the purpose-built CE software market (estimated)
- But their monopoly power is driving pricing up and feature development down
- Reviewers consistently say the product feels like "a work in progress"
- Post-acquisition integration is messy ‚Äî Destiny One and Lumens users are uncertain about the roadmap

### What's the Average Contract Value?
- **Enterprise (Destiny One tier):** $25,000‚Äì$75,000+/year (estimated; not published)
- **Mid-market (Lumens/Enrole/CampusCE tier):** $10,000‚Äì$30,000/year (estimated)
- **Budget (ACEware, CourseStorm for CE):** $3,000‚Äì$12,000/year
- Implementation fees are additional: $10,000‚Äì$50,000 for enterprise

### What Would Make Someone Switch?
From the review patterns and pain points, the top switching triggers are:
1. **Price increase** ‚Äî Modern Campus raising prices post-acquisition
2. **Poor support** ‚Äî feeling unheard or deprioritized
3. **Upgrade breaks critical workflow** ‚Äî immediate crisis trigger
4. **New leadership** at CE division who did the research elsewhere
5. **Modern UX** ‚Äî staff turnover brings in younger admins who demand better tools
6. **AI/automation promise** ‚Äî "we can save you 15+ hours/week" (Enrole's positioning)

### Is Anyone Building AI-Native in This Space?
**No. Not yet.**
- Ellucian's "Journey" has skills mapping that uses some intelligence, but it's not core registration
- CourseStorm added "AI-powered reporting" to their Premium tier ‚Äî likely basic
- No platform has: conversational enrollment assistant, predictive fill/cancel modeling, behavior-based marketing automation, intelligent program recommendations
- This is a **wide-open gap**

### Sales Cycle to Community Colleges?
- **Typical: 6‚Äì18 months** (6 months for smaller decisions, 12‚Äì18 months for enterprise)
- Decision-making process: CE Dean/Director identifies need ‚Üí IT evaluates ‚Üí Finance approves ‚Üí sometimes VP/Provost ‚Üí Board approval for large contracts
- RFP process often required for contracts >$10,000‚Äì$25,000 (varies by state)
- **Relationship-driven**: "Long-term relationships are essential. The worst thing a company can do is not retain their best representatives." (Inside Higher Ed)
- Conference presence (LERN, NCCET, AACC) is critical for discovery

### State-Level Purchasing Agreements That Matter
- **California:** FoundationCCC's CollegeBuys program ‚Äî negotiated technology contracts with "piggyback" provisions allowing other institutions to use the same pricing. Critical for the 115 California community colleges.
- **E&I Cooperative Services** ‚Äî nationwide non-profit cooperative for education procurement; getting on their contract list is a significant business accelerator
- **NASPO ValuePoint** ‚Äî another national cooperative purchasing program used by public institutions
- **Texas DIR (Department of Information Resources)** ‚Äî Texas colleges often use state contracts
- **Key insight:** Getting on a state cooperative contract can open doors to dozens of institutions at once. California alone has 115 community colleges.

## 6. Startup/New Entrant Analysis

### Who's Trying to Enter This Space?
- **Enrole (Entrinsik)** ‚Äî the most credible challenger; modern platform, Ellucian certified, Wake Tech as marquee customer
- **CampusCE** ‚Äî integration-focused, smaller operation
- No VC-funded startups have targeted this exact market (as of early 2026)
- General edtech startups (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, etc.) are in adjacent spaces but not CE registration

### Why Hasn't a Modern Startup Won Yet?
1. **Long sales cycles** ‚Äî 12‚Äì18 months to close deals; burns runway
2. **Integration requirements** ‚Äî must connect to Banner or Colleague; complex APIs
3. **Regulatory complexity** ‚Äî FERPA, PCI DSS, state compliance
4. **Niche market** ‚Äî ~1,000 prospects is small for a VC-backed startup
5. **Low awareness** ‚Äî CE directors are not reading TechCrunch; they're at LERN conferences
